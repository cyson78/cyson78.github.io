Who am I? Why am I here?

I took Computer Science in High School on a semi-dare, because no one thought I could achieve something that complicated. What I didn't anticipate out of that experience was that I'd actually enjoy it. I received a B.A. in Speech Language Pathology in December of 2008 because I thought that I was destined to work with kids the rest of my life. Because of that one computer science course, I knew I prefered computers over children as cute as they may be. I started out as a Customer Support/Account Manager supporting accounts post-sales and soon moved on to a Senior Technical Support position with an educational software company resolving installation issues, cloud support and troubleshooting something as simple as updating a roster, resetting passwords and adding new accounts. This soon changed into an environment for the survival of the fittest as a database analyst at a software start up in Plano in the automotive industry. Tough place to be, very challenging but worth it at the end. I decided I needed a change and worked up the courage to take a chance on an offer that had come across my lap more than once with a much larger, international company that caters to the hospitality industry working with hotels, hostels, bed & breakfast, motels, you name it, we have it. While I want to say that I'm heading down the right path, I am always open for new adventure and looking for new challenges and a forever home where I can continue advancing my technical skills. More coding, more challenges, tough roads to cross and new lands to discover. I'll be the first to admit, I'm not an expert, but I'd like to further continue my skills. This is what life is about.
